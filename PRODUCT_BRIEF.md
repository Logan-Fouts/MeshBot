# MeshBot Product Brief

## Overview

MeshBot is an offline field-assistance platform for parks departments operating in low-connectivity environments. It combines a Meshtastic-compatible radio node, local edge compute, and an on-device language model to answer short text questions when internet and cellular service are unavailable.

The intended use is not consumer novelty. The intended use is operational resilience for public lands.

## Buyer problem

US parks departments routinely manage:
- Trail systems with poor or no cellular service
- Seasonal staff coverage gaps
- Visitors who need basic safety guidance in remote areas
- Environments where radios work but broadband does not
- Pressure to improve public safety without installing expensive infrastructure everywhere

## MeshBot value proposition

MeshBot gives agencies a lightweight, offline guidance layer that can be placed close to the edge of the problem:
- Trailheads
- Ranger outposts
- Maintenance vehicles
- Backcountry shelters
- High-elevation relay points

It can help reduce friction around common informational requests such as:
- Basic first aid steps before higher-level help is available
- General navigation principles
- Heat, cold, smoke, wildlife, and dehydration guidance
- Equipment-use questions
- Park-program or site-specific information if curated locally

## Product package for agencies

A real sellable offering should be framed as a package, not just code.

Recommended package:
- Hardened edge device with weather-resistant enclosure
- Meshtastic radio and antenna kit
- Local model runtime with approved model selection
- Preconfigured prompt and policy pack
- Installation guide and seasonal operations SOP
- Support agreement with update cadence and replacement process
- Pilot success metrics and post-pilot review template

## What agencies care about in procurement

- Reliability in harsh field conditions
- Clear statement of what the system does not do
- Data retention and privacy posture
- Security of device access and radio channel use
- Ease of deployment by non-specialist staff
- Cost per site and annual support cost
- ADA and public communication considerations
- Compatibility with incident command and existing ranger workflows

## Pilot structure

Suggested first pilot:
- Duration: 60 to 90 days
- Scope: one park, one to three sites
- User population: visitors plus ranger staff in a controlled test window
- Success metrics: uptime, message completion rate, response usefulness, escalation appropriateness, support burden

## Pricing model ideas

Likely viable models:
- Hardware plus annual software support
- Annual site license per deployed node
- Managed pilot fee credited toward broader rollout

## Messaging for buyers

Use this framing:
- MeshBot extends guidance into coverage gaps.
- MeshBot improves resilience but does not replace emergency systems.
- MeshBot is designed for constrained, high-need environments where cloud-first tools are unreliable.

Avoid this framing:
- AI ranger replacement
- Emergency response substitute
- Medical advice system
- Real-time situational awareness platform unless that capability actually exists
